Iteration vs Recursion

In this section you will learn the differences between recursion and iteration, especially with regard to green coding.

What is recursion and iteration?

  • Iteration and recursion are multiple executions of one or more instructions.
  • With recursion, your algorithm calls itself either directly or via other functions.
  • The iteration is realized by loops (for, while). The loop is terminated by a termination condition.
  • Any recursion can also be converted into an iterative solution and vice versa.

What do recursion and iteration have to do with green code?

  • In programming, a decision must be made depending on the application as to whether iteration or recursion is used.
  • If this decision is made incorrectly, it can lead to serious disadvantages in energy consumption, runtime and thus the energy efficiency of your program.

How does the game operate in general?

The game consists of three sticks of the same size, A, B and C, on which several punched discs are placed, all different sizes. At the beginning, all the discs are on stick A, ordered by size, with the largest disc at the bottom and the smallest at the top. The aim of the game is to move the complete stack of discs from A to C. In this comparison, the power consumption and execution time were measured. The measurements were taken at an interval of 200 ms or 5 Hz, and the programme Towers of Hanoi with 30 hard disks was run. The recursive algorithm implemented in C++ consumed an average of 322.23 joules, while its iterative version consumed 1656.26 joules, an increase of more than 400 %. It turns out that it is not possible to say in principle whether recursion or iteration is more efficient in terms of energy consumption. The decision always depends on the situation.
